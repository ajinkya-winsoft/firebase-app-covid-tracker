Pre Lockdown Initial Phase

30 Jan 2020

First case of corona virus was confirmed in India in the state of Kerala.

19 March 2020

India reported 500 cases and PM Modi coined the Idea of Janta Curfew.

22 March 2020

All Indians observed the Janta Cerfew, a 14-hour long curfew scheduled from 7 am to 9pm.

Confirmed 571 Recovered 40 Deceased 10 First Phase 25 March – 14 April

25 March 2020

India initiated the largest lockdown in the history and 1.3 billion people saw the empty roads for the first time.

26 March 2020

Finance minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced a ₹170,000 crore (US$24 billion) stimulus package to help those affected by the lockdown

29 March 2020

Indian Railways announced that it would start services for special parcel trains to transport essential goods

5 April 2020

Citizens all over India cheered and showed solidarity with the health workers, police, and all those fighting the disease by switching off the electric lights at home from 9:00 p.m. to 9:09 p.m.for 9 minutes and lighting diya, candle; and flashing torchlight and mobile flashlight.

18 April 2020

Towards the end of the initial period, the rate of growth of COVID infections in India had significantly slowed, from a rate of doubling every three days prior to the lockdown to one of doubling every eight days

First time in 167 years India's rail network had been suspended. {{percentRecovered}}% of the total case were reported in this phase. 1325 people recovered as compared to only 10 in previous phase Confirmed 10914 Recovered 1325 Deceased 386 Second Phase Extension (15 April – 3 May)

16 April 2020

Lockdown areas were classified as
red zone, indicating the presence of infection hotspots.
orange zone, indicating some infection.
green zone, with no infections.

16 April 2020

The government also announced certain relaxations, allowing agricultural businesses, including dairy, aquaculture and plantations, as well as shops selling farming supplies, to open.

25 April 2020

Small retail shops were allowed to open with half the staff. Again social distancing norms were to be followed.

29 April 2020

The Ministry of Home Affairs issued guidelines for the states to allow inter-state movement of the stranded persons.

Confirmed 31293 Recovered 10398 Deceased 1067 Third Phase Extension (4 May – 17 May)

4 May 2020

The country has been split into 3 zones:
red zones (130 districts).
orange zones (284 districts).
green zones (319 districts).

Red zones are those with high coronavirus cases and a high doubling rate. Orange zones are those with comparatively fewer cases and green zones are those without any cases in the past 21 days. Normal movement is permitted in green zones with buses limited to 50 percent capacity. Orange zones would allow only private and hired vehicles but no public transportation. The zone classification would be revised once a week.
